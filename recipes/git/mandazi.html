Mandazi

Mandazi (East African Doughnuts) a spicy, airy yeast doughnut dough made with coconut milk, flavored with cardamom and grated fresh coconut or coconut flakes.

Ingredient

  • 2 1/4 teaspoon of yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 grated fresh coconut or coconut flakes
  • 3 1/2 to 4 cups of all purpose flour as needed
  • vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Activate yeast

    -In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. Prepare dough

    -In a large mixing bowl combine the flour, sugar, cardamom, and salt. Mix well.

    -Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the activated yeast mixture, coconut milk, and melted butter (or oil).

    -Mix until a sticky dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, gradually add more flour until it becomes manageable.

  3. Knead the Dough

    -Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface. Knead for about 5-7 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  4. First Rise

    -Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

  5. Shape the Mandazi

    -Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a floured surface. Roll the dough out to about 1/2 inch thickness.

    -Cut the dough into desired shapes (triangles or circles) using a knife or cookie cutter.

  6. Second Rise

    -Place the cut dough on a floured surface or parchment paper. Cover with a cloth and let them rise for an additional 30 minutes.

  7. Fry the Mandazi

    -Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to about 350°F (175°C). Carefully drop the dough pieces into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding.

    -Fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Use a slotted spoon to remove the Mandazi and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

  8. Serve

    -Let the Mandazi cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, dusted with powdered sugar or served with tea.
