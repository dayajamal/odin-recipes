Chapati

Description

Soft, golden flatbread made from whole wheat flour, cooked to perfection on a hot griddle. Ideal for pairing with curries and stews, offering a deliciously tender and versatile side.

Ingredient

  • 2 cups all purpose flour(or whole wheat flour)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 table spoon oil or ghee (makes chapati soft)
  • 3/4 cup warm water approximately

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough

    -In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.

    -If using, add the oil or ghee to the flour and mix it in with your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs.

    -Gradually add the warm water, a little at a time, while mixing the dough with your hand or a spoon. Continue until the dough starts to come together.

    -Knead the dough in the bowl or on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour. If it's too dry, add a bit more water.

    -Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 20-30 minutes. This helps the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out the chapatis.

  2. Shape the Chapatis

    -After resting, divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions (balls).

    -On a floured surface, flatten one ball of dough with your fingers, then roll it out into a thin circle about 6-8 inches in diameter. Use a rolling pin and keep the dough lightly floured to prevent sticking.

  3. Cook the Chapatis

    Heat a tava, griddle, or non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.

    Once hot, place the rolled-out chapati onto the skillet. Cook until you see bubbles forming on the surface, about 30 seconds to 1 minute

    Flip the chapati using tongs or a spatula. Cook the other side until small brown spots appear.

    For the traditional puffed effect, press gently on the chapati with a cloth or spatula. It should puff up. Flip it once more if needed.

    Remove from the skillet and place in a container lined with a cloth to keep warm and soft. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.

  4. Serve

    Serve the chapatis warm with curries, vegetables, or your favorite dishes.

    Tips

  • Consistency: The dough should be soft but not sticky. It should be pliable and easy to roll out.
  • Resting: Do not skip the resting time; it helps to make rolling easier and the chapatis softer.
  • Cooking: Make sure the skillet is hot enough for the chapatis to cook quickly and evenly.

    • Enjoy your chapatis as a versatile and tasty accompaniment to a variety of meals
